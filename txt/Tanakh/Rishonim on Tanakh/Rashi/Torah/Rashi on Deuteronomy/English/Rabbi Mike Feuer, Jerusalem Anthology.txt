all day long -  that is, forever. From the time Jerusalem was chosen, the Divine Presence has never dwelt elsewhere.
and dwells between his shoulders -  The Holy Temple was built on the highest point of his land, except that it was twenty-three cubits below the Eitam Spring (see Yoma 31a).  David intended to build it there, as is taught in Shechitath Kodashim [Zev. 54b]: But they said to David ‘Let us build it a little lower, because it is written ‘and He dwells between his shoulders,’ and there is no part of an ox more beautiful than its shoulders.’
